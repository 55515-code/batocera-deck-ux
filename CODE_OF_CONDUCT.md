# LuigiOS Community Code

LuigiOS is a place for people who care about games, art, repair, preservation,
and open computing. We want participation to feel curious, constructive, and
safe whether someone is writing kernel code, testing one controller, drawing an
icon, or asking their first Linux question.

The project puts game-device control in the hands of players while respecting
the people who make games, hardware, art, and software. Community members,
independent creators, upstream projects, and companies meet under the same
standards for consent, attribution, evidence, and technical review.

## Expected behavior

- Treat people with respect. Critique work and decisions, not identity or worth.
- Assume good intent while still naming concrete technical or community harm.
- Make room for beginners and communicate jargon with patience.
- Respect privacy, consent, attribution, licenses, and contributor boundaries.
- Disclose conflicts of interest and correct mistakes plainly.
- Keep feedback specific, actionable, and proportionate.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, threats, hate, sexualized attention, stalking, doxxing, impersonation,
spam, retaliation, or sustained disruption are not welcome. Neither are requests
for credentials, private keys, copyrighted payloads, or personal data.

Do not use LuigiOS spaces to upload or trade ROMs, BIOS images, console firmware,
decryption keys, account tokens, or other material you do not have permission to
distribute. Discussion of lawful preservation, interoperability, emulator
configuration, and user-owned migration is welcome.

## Scope and enforcement

This code applies in project repositories, issue trackers, chat servers, social
communities, events, and private project communications. Moderators may clarify,
edit, hide, remove, slow, lock, mute, or ban participation to protect the space.
Good-faith correction is preferred when practical; serious or repeated harm can
move directly to removal or a ban.

Report community conduct concerns privately to the moderator team for the
platform where they occurred. Report software vulnerabilities through GitHub's
private Security Advisory form, as described in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
Moderators will limit disclosure to people needed to review the report, avoid
conflicts of interest, document enforcement actions, and provide an appeal path
to a maintainer who did not make the original decision when possible.
