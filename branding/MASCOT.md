# LuigiOS Mascot States
The LuigiOS caretaker is always the same raccoon. Clothing, facial markings,
body proportions, technical role, and calm competence remain stable across
variants. Angel and demon cues communicate interface risk; they are not
separate characters and do not represent good or bad users.

## Angel variant

Use `assets/mascot/caretaker-angel.png` for recommended defaults, successful
validation, backups, restore points, safe mode, rollback, and recoverable
actions. The halo and wings reinforce a protected path.

## Demon variant

Use `assets/mascot/caretaker-demon.png` for developer controls, experimental
features, destructive operations, unsigned content warnings, and actions that
cannot be automatically reversed. The horns and red rim light signal elevated
risk without presenting the caretaker as hostile.

## Interaction rules

- Never use a mascot variant as the only status or warning indicator. Pair it
  with a plain-language label, semantic icon, and appropriate confirmation.
- Use the angel only after a path is actually safer or recoverable. Do not use
  it to imply that community content has been security-reviewed.
- Use the demon sparingly at decision points, not as ambient decoration or a
  punishment for enabling advanced settings.
- Preserve controller focus, text contrast, and dialog hierarchy when the art
  is present. Hide the art before shrinking required controls or copy.
- Keep both variants optional in reduced-motion, high-contrast, low-memory, and
  minimal-install profiles.
